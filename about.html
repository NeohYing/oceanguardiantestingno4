Frequently Asked Questions

Why are marine animals becoming endangered?

Marine animals are becoming endangered due to threats such as pollution, overfishing, habitat destruction, and climate change. These human activities damage ecosystems and reduce the resources marine species need to survive. Without protection, some species may face extinction.

What can I do to help protect the oceans?

Everyone can make a difference by single-use plastics, recycling properly, conserving water, and participating in beach clean-ups. Supporting conservation organizations and spreading awareness can also help protect marine ecosystems.

Why are oceans important to humans?

Oceans produce half of the world's oxygen and help regulate Earth's climate. They provide food, jobs, and resources for billions of people. Healthy oceans are essential for both environmental and human well-being.

Can endangered marine species recover?

Yes. Through habitat protection, reduced pollution, and conservation programs, many endangered marine species have shown signs of recovery. For example, protected areas and stricter fishing regulations have helped some populations increase over time. However, continued conservation efforts and public awareness are essential to ensure their long-term survival.
